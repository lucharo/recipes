Ingredients:  
1 can of white beans, drained or 1 1/2 cup cooked white beans   
4 medium green garlic stalks   
3 tbsp olive oil plus more to garnish   
Zest of 1 lemon  
Juice of 1/2 lemon  
1/2-1 tsp salt   
1 small bunch dandelion greens  
  
Preparation:  
1. Separate the white part and green part of the green garlic. Slice white part vertically. Cut green part into 2 inch pieces.   
2. Bring a heavy bottomed pan to high heat with a generous amount of neutral oil. Sear the white part of the green garlic until charred on all sides but not burnt. Season lightly with salt. Deglaze with 1/4 cup water and turn off heat. Set aside.  
3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and season with salt. Add the green part of the green garlic and blanch for 2 minutes. Transfer to ice bath to cool then strain and dry.   
4. In the same part add the dandelion greens and blanch for 2-3 minutes or until stems are tender. Transfer to ice bath to cool then strain and dry.  
5. In a food processor combine the white beans, seared green garlic bottoms, blanched green garlic tops, lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, and salt. Process until smooth. Taste and adjust salt if desired.  
6. Garnish with dandelion greens, olive oil, and flakey salt.
