1. Congee or Jook (粥) - savoury rice porridge. Almost every asian country has their own version of this. I like to cook mine down until all the rice has almost disintegrated. I go light in flavour for the actual porridge but heavy on the toppings. Non-negotiable toppings for me are: white pepper, fresh hot chilli in soy sauce and Chinese fried breadsticks. I also like preserved red beancurd (very salty umami pasty cubes) and soy pickled cucumbers.   
2. Cheung Fun (肠粉) - Chinese-Style Steamed Rice Noodle Rolls. I like to pan fry these once they’re steamed (I just buy them fresh from my local Vietnamese grocer) - it takes a while so be patient. I serve them up Cantonese-style, the way I ate them growing up, with Hoisin Sauce mixed with a little agave syrup and sesame oil, and melted smooth peanut butter. Optional to top with toasted sesame seeds, fresh spring onions, crispy shallots or chilli oil.  
3. Bánh Cuốn - Vietnamese-Style Steamed Rice Noodle Rolls. Technically the same noodle rolls as Cheung Fun but served differently. These Bánh Cuốn are filled with shredded woodear mushroom and mince before being rolled up. Served with nước chấm and a sprinkling of crispy fried shallots.
