New sauces are dropping in the new year! 🌶️   
  
For now use your own homemade or store brought, you might need to use more or less marinade depending on how spicey it is, or sweet or acidic so trust your taste buds!  
  
OR do a simple soy glaze written below x  
  
Servs 6 - 8  
  
1kg Lions Mane  
6 tbsp j marinade  
Olive Oil  
Salt  
  
Gravy  
4tbsp j marinade  
200 ml dark drum  
500ml veg stock  
1 tbsp vegan butter (I use nuturli)  
2 tsp dark soy sauce  
2 tbsp corn flour + 4 tbsp water  
  
This will vary depending on the marinade you use some need more sweetness some need more acidity- taste as you cook!  
1 tsp red wine vinegar  
1 tsp agave  
  
Simple Soy Glaze  
1kg lions mane  
4 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)  
2 tbsp maple syrup   
2 tbsp olive oil  
1 tbsp balsamic vinegar  
1 tsp Dijon mustard  
2 cloves garlic, minced  
2 sprigs of fresh rosemary  
Zest of 1 lemon (optional, for brightness)  
season with salt and pepper to taste  
  
METHOD  
Sear the Mushrooms:  
  
Start by heating a skillet on medium-high. Add the Lion’s Mane mushrooms and cook them without oil to release any excess water. Once they start to dry out, drizzle in some olive oil and sear until golden brown and crispy on the outside.  
Glaze and Roast:  
  
Whip up a glaze: mix soy sauce or tamari, fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme, a little minced garlic, and a touch of maple syrup for sweetness. Brush this glaze generously over the seared mushrooms. Pop them in the oven at 375°F (190°C) and roast until charred and caramelized on the outside—about 15–20 minutes.  
Serve It Up:  
  
Let the mushrooms cool slightly before slicing into thick, meaty pieces. Arrange them as a centerpiece, or pair them with your favorite festive sides. They’re rich, flavorful, and an absolute crowd-pleaser!  
Lion’s Mane mushrooms bring all the meaty goodness without any meat. They’re hearty, umami-packed, and guaranteed to impress at your Christmas feast. Cheers to a vegan holiday done right!
