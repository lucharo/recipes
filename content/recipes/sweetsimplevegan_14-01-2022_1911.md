Perfect vegan + gf flatbread ready in about 30 minutes! It’s great as an appetizer and also perfect to use as a base for pizza 🍕  
  
Recipe below and on https://sweetsimplevegan.com/2020/07/5-ingredient-gluten-free-flatbread 😋  
  
Ingredients  
* 1 1/3 cups (240g) unsweetened plain vegan yogurt (I used Forager)  
* 8 tsp (36g) olive oil  
* 2 tsp (9g) baking powder  
* 1 tsp (9g) salt  
* 2 cups + 1 1/2 tbsp (314g) Bob’s Red Mill 1:1 Gluten-free Baking Flour + more as needed  
  
Optional  
* Garlic olive oil  
* Fresh parsley, chopped   
  
Instructions  
1. In a large bowl, mix the vegan yogurt, olive oil, baking powder and salt.  
2. Sprinkle in the gf flour and mix it through until a dough forms. You want a smooth and soft dough that does not crumble and is not too sticky. Add additional flour or water, 1/2 tbsp at a time, as needed.  
3. Flour a clean work surface and preheat a greased pan over medium heat. We prefer to use a cast iron griddle or skillet vs. a non-stick pan. We also prefer to use our granite counters, a marble board or plastic cutting boards instead of a wooden one as it sticks less.  
4. Divide the dough into 8 balls. Place 1 ball on the floured work surface and using a rolling pin, roll out the dough until it is about 1/4-inch thick. Transfer the dough onto the heated pan and cook for 1 1/2 to 2 1/2 minutes on each, or until browned and cooked through. Check the dough after 1 1/2 minutes to ensure that it is not burning. If it cooks too fast, lower your heat. We often find that our first flatbread becomes the test for proper heat level/cooking time and the remaining flatbreads cook more and successfully.  
5. Transfer the flatbread to a plate. Brush it with garlic olive oil and give it a generous sprinkle of parsley.  
6. Continue until you have cooked all of the dough. Serve warm. Store in an airtight container for 4-6 days.  
  
Notes  
* All gf flour blends are different so we cannot guarantee that another blend will work.  
* Regular AP flour can work in place of the gf flour.  
* You can use any other oil in place of the olive oil.  
* You can mix seasonings or herbs into the dough as well!
