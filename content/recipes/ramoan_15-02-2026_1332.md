A complete plate.  
  
Recipe below for when you need something grounding, rich and reliable.  
  
300g red lentils  
1 tsp sea salt  
½ tsp turmeric  
1 tbsp cumin seeds  
1 tsp cardamom pods, crushed  
2 onions, finely chopped  
2 bay leaves  
1 cinnamon stick  
5 garlic cloves, minced  
300g tomatoes, chopped  
½ scotch bonnet or chilli  
200ml coconut milk  
1 tbsp garam masala  
Fresh coriander, chopped  
1 tbsp vegan butter  
  
	  
Rinse the red lentils until the water runs clear. Place in a saucepan and add water so it sits roughly 1cm above the lentils. Bring to the boil and cook for around 15 minutes until soft. Add sea salt and turmeric, stirring occasionally and adding more water if needed.  
  
In a separate pan, heat vegan ghee or oil over a low–medium heat. Add cumin seeds and crushed cardamom pods and allow to toast briefly. Add onions, bay leaves and the cinnamon stick. Sauté until the onions soften, then add the garlic and cook for a further few minutes.  
  
Add the chopped tomatoes and chilli and cook down for 3–4 minutes. Stir in the cooked lentils, followed by the coconut milk, garam masala, fresh coriander and vegan butter. Allow to simmer gently for 6–7 minutes. Season to taste and serve.
