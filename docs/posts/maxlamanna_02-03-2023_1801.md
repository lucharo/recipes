Over the last 2 years, I’ve been told that most people here waste:  
Bread  
Aubergine/eggplant  
Celery  
  
Hopefully this caponata-inspired dish helps you waste less food and save some money!  
  
Steps:  
1 aubergine/eggplant, cubed  
1 tbsp olive oil  
Pinch of salt  
  
Roast in the oven for 30-35 minutes at 180C/350F fan  
  
1 tbsp olive oil  
1 onion, diced  
1 red pepper, chopped  
2 celery stalks, thinly sliced  
Pinch of salt and black pepper  
2 garlic cloves, minced  
400g/14 oz crushed tomatoes  
Handful of green olives, roughly chopped  
Handful raisins  
3 tbsp red wine vinegar  
2 tbsp capers  
1 tsp sugar  
1/4 tsp red pepper flakes  
1 bay leaf  
Chopped parsley  
Fresh basil  
  
Cook the onion, red pepper and celery for a few minutes until soft, a pinch of salt and black pepper. Then add you garlic and stir, cook for 1 minute.   
  
Next, add the remaining ingredients, stir  and cook on medium heat for 8-10 minutes. Remove the bay leaf at the end and stir in the roasted aubergine and fresh parsley.  
  
Half baguette, sliced - drizzle a bit of olive oil and salt, bake in the oven for 5-7 minutes until warm and crusty.  
  
4 tbsp pine nuts - toast in a frying pan over medium heat, occasionally tossing, until lightly brown.  
   
Serves 4-6, great as leftovers and is freezer friendly.
