# DIP O’CLOCK! Squash dip with gochujang and homemade sesame paste.  

> recipe by [@pierceabernathy](https://www.instagram.com/pierceabernathy/) 
(Pierce Abernathy) - [see original post](https://instagram.com/p/Cikh3wJAw-o)

![](../img/pierceabernathy_16-09-2022_1426.png)

  
Gochujang is a Korean fermented chili paste. It is a fundamental ingredient in Korean cooking and has a deep, rich, spicy and slightly sweet flavor. The homemade sesame paste is inspired by Chinese sesame paste. Not to be confused with tahini, which typically the sesame is not toasted or very lightly toasted. This paste has a deeper roasty flavor compared to tahini. The two are not typically interchangeable.   
  
NOTE: Different types of squash contain different amounts of water. For example, summer squash is around 95% water while butternut squash is around 85%. This particular squash contained quite a bit so in order to thicken the dip I mixed in some breadcrumbs and allowed the dip to rest in the fridge for 2-3 hours before processing one final time to fully incorporate. Depending on the cooked squash texture and texture of the dip you may not have to do this.   
  
Ingredients:   
2 cups cooked squash  
2 tbsp gochujang  
2 tsp rice vinegar  
1 tsp soy sauce  
1” piece of ginger, grated  
  
1/2 cup toasted sesame seeds   
Water  
1 clove garlic, grated  
1 tbsp lemon juice  
Salt to taste  
  
Toasted sesame seeds to garnish  
Sesame oil to garnish   
  
Preparation:  
1. On a parchment-lined baking sheet, season squash with neutral oil and salt. Roast at 400F/200C until tender, about 30-40 minutes. Allow to cool.   
2. In a food processor or mortar and pestle, add the sesame seeds and mix until broken down into the texture of wet sand. Slowly add water and continue to mix until paste forms. Add the garlic, lemon juice and salt. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.   
3. For the squash dip, add the cooked squash, gochujang, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and ginger to a food processor. Process until fully combined. (Add 1/4-1/2 cup breadcrumbs at this point if texture of dip is too loose).  
4. Serve squash dip in shallow bowl with sesame paste, toasted sesame seeds and sesame oil.
