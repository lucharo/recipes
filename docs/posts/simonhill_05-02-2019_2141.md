# This one’s for my folks concerned about protein deficiency 😆 

> recipe by [@simonhill](https://www.instagram.com/simonhill/) 
(Simon J Hill, MSc, BSc) - [see original post](https://instagram.com/p/Btg_qqEH2_u)

![](../img/simonhill_05-02-2019_2141.png)

⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
61.7g of Protein and 30g of fibre in this delicious chickpea pasta (swipe right for ingredients). Total energy provided by this meal is 670 calories. You can lower it without sacrificing flavour by reducing sweet potato & tempeh serving size. ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
The pasta sauce is a mix of sweet potato purée (bake then scoop out), vegan mayo and siracha (find a clean ingredient version without added sugar and sodium or use chilli instead). It’s a game changing pasta sauce trust me 😋 and for kids or anyone that doesn’t like it spicy I would suggest removing the siracha. Also for anyone wanting a creamier sauce add a little bit of your favourite plant milk when you blitz this sauce together in your blender. Once blended for those who like purple cabbage and want to load up the antioxidant value of this meal, chop up 1/4 of a cup or so and throw into the sauce. ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
Cook the pasta. Drain and mix through pasta sauce. Whilst pasta is cooking pan fry mushrooms, tomatoes and tempeh (cubed into tiny bite size pieces). In reality add whatever other vegetables you want - I’m going to keep on saying it, diversity of plant foods across your weeks is crucial and I cannot stress this enough. ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀  
To get some greens into meal I often add spinach to the pan right add the end so it’s just lightly heated. Top with basil, pink salt, pepper and nutritional yeast 🤙🏼🔥 ⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀⠀
